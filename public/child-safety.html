Child Safety Standards

Last updated: July 19, 2026

Elix Star Live Ltd ("we", "us", "our"), registered in England and Wales, operates the Elix Star Live application. We are committed to the safety of children and to preventing child sexual abuse and exploitation (CSAE) on our platform. This document sets out our published standards against CSAE, in line with Google Play's Child Safety Standards policy.

1. Zero-Tolerance Policy

We have a strict zero-tolerance policy toward child sexual abuse material (CSAM) and any content or behaviour that sexualises, endangers, or exploits children. This includes, without limitation:

  • Any depiction of child sexual abuse material (CSAM)
  • Sexualisation of minors in images, video, live streams, audio, text, or chat
  • Grooming, solicitation, or sextortion of minors
  • Trafficking, endangerment, or any other exploitation of children
  • Inappropriate contact between adults and minors

Violations result in immediate content removal, permanent account termination, and reporting to the relevant authorities.

2. Minimum Age Requirement

Elix Star Live is not intended for children under 13. Users must be at least 13 years old (or the minimum digital age of consent in their country, whichever is higher) to create an account. We do not knowingly allow anyone under the minimum age to use the App, and we remove accounts that we identify as belonging to underage users.

3. Preventing and Detecting Abuse

  • Content moderation across uploaded videos, live streams, profiles, and chat
  • Automated and manual review of reported content
  • Prompt removal of violating content and suspension of offending accounts
  • Community Guidelines that expressly prohibit CSAE and endangerment of minors

4. In-App Reporting

Every user can report content, profiles, live streams, and messages directly within the App using the built-in Report feature. Reports concerning child safety are prioritised and reviewed as quickly as possible. Users can also contact us directly (see Contact below) to report child safety concerns.

5. Response and Enforcement

When we become aware of CSAE or CSAM on our platform, we:

  • Remove the content immediately
  • Permanently terminate the responsible account(s)
  • Preserve relevant evidence in accordance with applicable law
  • Report to the National Center for Missing & Exploited Children (NCMEC) and/or relevant regional or national authorities, such as the UK's Internet Watch Foundation (IWF) and law enforcement, as required by law

6. Compliance with Child Safety Laws

We comply with applicable child safety laws in the jurisdictions in which we operate, and we cooperate with law enforcement and child protection organisations. We report apparent CSAM to the appropriate regional and national authorities.
